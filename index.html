About the Summit

South Africa & Russia:
Where Deals Are Done

Trade Week SA–Russia is a landmark bilateral summit bringing together government officials, business leaders, and agricultural experts from both nations to forge lasting economic partnerships grounded in mutual value.

Studex Global Markets is building Africa's answer to Davos — not a conference, but a permanent bilateral deal engine where governments and enterprises come to execute. The "glue" that connects partners, and the "local arm" that extends them into new geographies. Trade Week is where that engine fires for the first time.

As Tumelo Ramaphosa, Founder & CEO, puts it: "The platform is free. The deals carry the economics." This summit is the live expression of the Studex Dual-Mode Deal Engine: five active synergies, USD 88–198M lifetime pipeline, Deputy Minister of Agriculture as confirmed chair.

Studex Meat · Our Legacy

Trading Our Heritage
to the World

For over 2,000 years, African herdsmen tended the Ankole — one of the world's most ancient and distinguished cattle breeds. With dramatically sweeping horns and a lean, flavourful carcass, the Ankole carries the DNA of a continent's pastoral legacy.

Studex Meat exists to take that legacy global. Alongside the revered Japanese Wagyu — the world's most premium beef — Studex brings African excellence to international markets, beginning with Russia.

The meat trade corridor to Russia, chaired at this summit by the Deputy Minister of Agriculture, is not just commerce. It is the continuation of a 2,000-year story.

Sports Diplomacy

The Mandela African Boxing Cup

IBA-sanctioned. 41 nations. Hosted by Studex in Durban. The world's premier Africa-hosted international boxing event — and a vehicle for real business between nations.

The Mandela African Boxing Cup is more than sport — it is Studex's strategic vehicle for building state-level relationships between South Africa, Russia, and the broader African continent. With 41 nations competing and the full backing of the IBA, SA Minister of Sports Zizi Kodwa, and IBA President Umar Kremlev, the event represents the intersection of sport, culture, and commerce that defines the Studex philosophy.
